Five Helpful Facts About Copyright Law

1.

A copyright grants you many exclusive rights including: creating derivative works based on the work, distributing copies of the work, and publicly displaying the work.



2.

For works created on or after January 1, 1978 the duration of a copyright lasts from the moment of its creation and lasts for the author’s life plus an additional 70 years.



3.

There are a few things that even copyright law can’t protect: ideas, fashion designs, methods, and systems.



4.

Myth: Do not assume that a work is in the public domain or that a work is not protected because a copyright symbol is not shown. You can look up registered works on the Copyright Office website to determine whether or not you may use the work.



5.

Work does not have to be published anywhere in order to be copyrighted; even unpublished works are eligible for copyright protection.



Fun Fact:

Generally sound recordings are copyrighted separately from the copyright of a musical composition as they are not considered the same work under copyright law.
